# Employee Attrition Prediction using Machine Learning

## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations as it increases recruitment costs, training expenses, and impacts productivity. This project aims to predict employee attrition using Machine Learning and provide actionable business insights through an interactive Power BI dashboard.

The project combines data analysis, machine learning, model explainability, and business intelligence to help HR departments identify employees at risk of leaving and take preventive measures.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Predict whether an employee is likely to leave the organization.
- Identify the key factors contributing to employee attrition.
- Visualize employee trends using Power BI.
- Generate actionable HR recommendations to reduce attrition.

---

## 📂 Dataset

- **Dataset:** IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset
- **Source:** IBM HR Analytics Dataset (Kaggle)
- **Records:** 1470 Employees
- **Target Variable:** Attrition (Yes/No)

## 🤖 Machine Learning Model

### Model Used

- Logistic Regression

### Model Evaluation

| Metric | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Accuracy | **89.8%** |
| Precision | **0.71** |
| Recall | **0.38** |

## 💡 Key Business Insights

- Research & Development and Sales departments experience the highest attrition.
- Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives have the highest turnover.
- Employees working overtime are more likely to leave.
- Employees aged 26–35 show the highest attrition.
- Attrition is highest during the early years of employment.
- Lower job satisfaction is associated with higher attrition.

---

## 📌 Business Recommendations

- Reduce excessive overtime.
- Improve work-life balance.
- Provide competitive compensation.
- Offer clear career progression opportunities.
- Conduct regular employee engagement surveys.
- Focus retention efforts on high-risk departments and job roles.
